Featured Work

Selected infrastructure and research projects in robotics data, scalable evaluation systems, and safety-critical testing.

Sentinel — Automated Red-Teaming & Safety Evaluation Platform for Vision-Language Models
(Technical report)

A distributed evaluation platform that stress-tests vision and vision-language models under structured real-world perturbations. Built with Ray-based parallelism, multi-signal failure detectors, and automated HTML/CSV/TXT reporting for reproducible audits.

Robotics Perception & Data Infrastructure — xLab (Safe Autonomous Systems Lab) / University of Pennsylvania

Built pipelines and tooling for multi-camera perception deployments: data ingestion, labeling workflows, dataset QA, and robustness evaluation (occlusion, lighting variation, sensor noise) to improve reliability in real-world settings.

StreamFlow — Multimodal Robotics Data Engine (In Progress)

A data engine that converts raw robot video and sensor logs into aligned, queryable, training-ready datasets. Designed as a reusable infrastructure layer for robotics teams, with scalable ingestion, timestamp alignment, metadata indexing, and automated quality checks.

Kubernetes-Deployed ML Inference Service (FastAPI + Ray)

Production-style ML inference service combining FastAPI and Ray, deployed on Kubernetes. Designed to support concurrent request handling with explicit backpressure, health probes, and rolling updates, and instrumented with Prometheus-style metrics to monitor latency, throughput, and system health under bursty load.

VidFlow — Scalable Robotics Video Compression & Evaluation Pipeline
(Technical report)

Ray-parallel benchmarking pipeline that quantifies compression–quality tradeoffs for robotics perception video. Measures perceptual and semantic degradation (PSNR/SSIM/LPIPS/CLIP) to inform storage and ingestion defaults at scale.

Serial Load: Visual Safety Benchmark for Vision-Language Models
(Paper)

Controlled benchmark probing how increasing visual reasoning load affects safety judgments, highlighting uncertainty and rare false-safe failures missed by standard accuracy metrics.
