Featured Work

Selected infrastructure and research projects focused on robotics data, scalable evaluation, and safety testing.

Sentinel — Automated Red-Teaming & Safety Evaluation Platform for Vision-Language Models
(Technical report)

A distributed evaluation platform that stress-tests vision and vision-language models under real-world perturbations. Built with Ray-based parallelism, multi-signal failure detectors, and automated HTML/CSV/TXT reporting for reproducible audits.

Robotics Perception & Data Infrastructure — xLab (Safe Autonomous Systems Lab) / University of Pennsylvania

End-to-end pipelines for multi-camera perception and robotics deployment: data ingestion, auto-labeling, training, inference, and robustness analysis. Focused on reliability in real-world settings (occlusion, lighting variation, sensor noise) and safety-oriented evaluation.

StreamFlow — Multimodal Robotics Data Engine (In Progress)

A data engine that converts raw robot video and sensor logs into aligned, queryable, training-ready datasets. Designed as a reusable infrastructure layer for fleet-scale embodied AI, with scalable ingestion, metadata, and quality checks.

VidFlow — Scalable Robotics Video Compression & Evaluation Pipeline
(Technical report)

Ray-parallel benchmarking pipeline that quantifies compression–quality tradeoffs for robotics perception video. Measures perceptual and semantic degradation (PSNR/SSIM/LPIPS/CLIP) to inform storage and ingestion defaults at scale.

Serial Load: Visual Safety Benchmark for Vision-Language Models
(Paper)

Controlled benchmark probing how increasing visual reasoning load affects safety judgments, highlighting uncertainty and rare false-safe failures missed by standard accuracy metrics.
